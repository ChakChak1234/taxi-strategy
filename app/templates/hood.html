The game of taxi pickups and dropoffs is played on a spacetime board of NYC. The probability of pickups, moves, and rewards is determined by the historical taxi data.



The game can be abstracted as a Markov Decision Process. Taking a page from computer-chess programs, we can use look-ahead trees to determine optimal next moves.



We can also use machine learning to analyze the historical data and identify hotspots for picking up profitable fares.
