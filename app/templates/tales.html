Over 500,000 taxi trips are made in NYC every day.
We can make a scatter plot of pickup and dropoff locations, which are recorded by GPS for each trip.



The GPS location data is extremely detailed, allowing us to zoom in on areas of interest.



However, we can see that the GPS accuracy noticeably suffers in midtown Manhattan (most likely due to the signals bouncing off of skyscrapers).



The data also contains detailed temporal information for each trip. The heartbeat of Manhattan is clearly evident.



Things are quieter over in Brooklyn. The GPS accuracy is detailed enough to show that pickups and dropoffs happen on opposite sides of the street.



Events at Barclays Center induce clear spikes in the time series of pickups and dropoffs.



We can also combine the spatial and temporal information.
